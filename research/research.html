Research

My research focuses on the effects that leadership and loyalty have on relationships and behavior in organizational contexts.

  • In the area of leadership, power and status (i.e., the vertical axis of the interpersonal circumplex) I build on the experiences and insights I gained working with powerful executives in the corporate world. I investigate:
    • The effect that power has on leaders, on their interactions with each other and on their performance in top teams, [Link]
    • The effect that leaders’ power and influence styles have on the cultures they create in their organizations, [Link]
    • The effect that status has more broadly on an individual’s psychological well-being. [Link]
  • In the area of loyalty (i.e., the horizontal axis of the interpersonal circumplex) I build on the insights of moral psychology to investigate:
    • The effects that loyalty has on individual ethical behavior [Link]
    • The expectations that loyalty gives rise to including and their effects of thoughts, feelings and behavior. E.g., expectations trust and trustworthiness, [Link]

My research is biased towards measuring actual behavior. I use both laboratory experiments to infer causality and field studies to demonstrate the generalizability of the effects I find in real-world contexts.

I also love to collaborate with other inspiring researchers on one-off exciting projects:

  • With Professors Will Self, Barb Mellers, Greg Mitchell and Phillip Tetlock, we investigated the effects of voice on shared circumstances [Link]
  • With Professors Don Moore and Steve Blader, we investigated how accountability affects implicit biases [Link]
