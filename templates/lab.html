Overview

Welcome to our research lab!

We are a social psychology research lab based at the Haas School of Business at UC Berkeley. We are a group of undergraduate students and a graduate student who work on organizational research for a number of faculty at Haas and at other US business schools.

Our research focuses on two main areas:

  1. the social psychology of power and status in organizational contexts. Particularly, we seek to understand the effects of power and status on leaders, on their interactions with their peers in top teams, and on the cultures they create in their organizations.
  2. the effects of loyalty on ethical decision making and trust. I.e., we seek to understand how loyalty gives rise to expectations that affect decision making and ethical behavior.

Our lab fosters a learning environment in which our undergraduate researchers are expected to continue to learn though-out their time in the lab and to get involved in all aspects of the research process. [See Values]

Since 2009, more than 50 undergraduate researchers have been part of the lab and the first few cohorts are now graduating and going onto successful careers in the corporate, medical and academic arenas.

How to apply

We hire new researchers every semester through the Berkeley Undergraduate Research Apprentice Program (URAP) link. Our research lab is listed under Professor Cameron Anderson at the Haas School - "Leadership in Unusual Contexts."   

We usually recruit a couple of new Research Apprentices each semester to join our research lab. We look for motivated, hard-working researchers who will bring a variety of different experiences and insights to our lab. We particularly value diversity: current researchers are majoring in business, economics, psychology, molecular biology, computer science, math, statistics and political science. Past alumni from our lab have gone on to graduate schools (MS, JD, MD, PhD), to work in finance (investment banking, accounting, consulting), and to work in the public and non-profit sectors (US government, the Red Cross).

  Our goal is to have students participate in as much of the research process as possible. Research Apprentices work on a variety of different research projects with a number of faculty and PhD students at Haas (and at other universities). Current projects relate to issues of power, status, close relationships, gratitude, ethics and conflict.

  Over the course of the semester Research Assistants will receive regular training on standard statistical tools including basic statistical software used in research analysis as well as survey and excel which have proved useful skills in subsequent internships and employment.

  Over the longer-term, many research apprentices stay on in the lab. We expect researchers to continue to learn and develop throughout their tenure in the lab: to become competent with technical and qualitative research skills, with communication and teamwork skills, and to take increasing responsibility for research projects, ultimately assuming leadership roles and reporting directly to faculty.

We run regular training on the research process, excel for beginners, intermediate, and vba, Managing Data, Qualtrics, Mechanical Turk, and SPSS.

For those new to Cal or to research, a desire to learn about research, theory, or both is the only prerequisite to joining the team. To do so as a part of Angus' team is an excellent opportunity to receive hands on training and practice developing analytical skills in an open, safe, friendly, and communicative environment where differing opinions are welcomed and every member of the team makes a substantive impact.
