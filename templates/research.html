Research: Leadership, power and status in groups and top teams

Leadership, Power and Status

Key Questions

  • Why do top teams perform poorly and how can performance issues be mitigated?
  • How does a leader’s power and influence style affect the performance of their groups and the cultures they create?
  • What is the nature of social status? And, how does it affect social functioning and well-being in groups?

Relevant Research

  • Anderson, C., Hildreth, J.A.D., & Howland, L. (2015) Do people possess a fundamental desire for status? A review of the empirical literature. Psychological Bulletin
  • Hildreth, J.A.D., Anderson, C. Failure at the top: How power undermines collaborative performance. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology. (2nd Revised and Resubmitted)
  • Chatman, J.A., Hildreth J.A.D. & Anderson, C. Do as I say not as I do: How leaders’ interpersonal style and power influence organizational culture.
  • Anderson, C. & Hildreth, J.A.D. Status and Subjective Wellbeing.

Research: Loyalty, ethics, morality, and trust in group life

Key Questions

  • What is the nature of loyalty? And how does it drive individual behavior in groups?
  • What are the effects of loyalty on expectations and acts of trust and trustworthiness?
  • Does loyalty corrupt?

Relevant Research

  • Hildreth, J.A.D., Gino, F., & Bazerman M. Blind loyalty? How group loyalty makes us see evil. Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes
  • Hildreth, J.A.D & Anderson, C. The Morality of Loyalty: How loyalty shapes ethical behavior.

Research: Group Behavior

My research focuses on group interactions and measures actual behavior whenever possible.

We bring executives and graduate students into the lab and video-tape group interactions.

We measure behaviors such as Agreement, Cheating, Creativity and Performance.
