Research

Social relationships are organized along two primary dimensions: “communion,” which reflects how cohesive and close individuals are with each other, and “power,” which reflects the degree to which individuals are hierarchically differentiated. My research examines how both of these primary dimensions can affect cognition, behavior, and performance at the individual and group levels.

Research: Loyalty, ethics, morality, and trust in group life

Loyalty and Ethics

Key Questions

  • What is the nature of loyalty? And how does it drive individual behavior in groups?
  • What are the effects of loyalty on expectations and acts of trust and trustworthiness?
  • Does loyalty corrupt?

Research: Power, status and leadership in groups and top teams

Key Questions

  • Why do top teams perform as poorly as they do? And how can performance issues be mitigated?
  • How does a leader’s power and influence style affect the performance of their groups and the cultures they create?
  • What is the nature of social status? And, how does it affect social functioning and well-being in groups?

Relevant Research

  • Hildreth, J.A.D. & Anderson, C. (in press) Failure at the top: How power undermines collaborative performance. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology.
